01 — Two Properties, One Limit

Chirality and Helicity Are Not the Same

For massive particles, chirality and helicity are distinct. Chirality is an algebraic property: it specifies which representation of the Lorentz group the field transforms under. A left-chiral electron belongs to the (½, 0) representation; a right-chiral electron to (0, ½). This assignment is intrinsic. No change of reference frame can alter it.

Helicity is different. It is the projection of a particle's spin onto its direction of motion: the sign of J·p. For a massive particle, helicity is frame-dependent. An observer who overtakes the particle sees its momentum reverse while its spin remains unchanged. What was left-handed becomes right-handed. Helicity is a kinematic accident. Chirality is a structural fact. The two concepts live in different categories.

For massless particles, the categories collapse. No boost can overtake a photon. No frame exists in which its momentum reverses. Helicity becomes Lorentz invariant. In the massless limit the Dirac spinor decomposes into independent Weyl spinors and the chiral projector ½(1−γ⁵) converges with the helicity projector. Chirality is helicity.

This identification is the thread connecting everything that follows. The Majorana question asks whether chirality alone distinguishes neutrinos from antineutrinos. The see-saw mechanism explains why the left-handed neutrino is light. Sterile neutrino dark matter would make the invisible universe right-handed. And Penrose's conformal cosmology imagines an endgame in which all mass evaporates, every surviving particle travels at the speed of light, and the distinction between chirality and helicity dissolves universally. These are research directions, not conclusions. What follows is a set of open questions connected by a single theme: what happens to handedness when mass goes away.

02 — The Particle That Is Its Own Mirror

Majorana Neutrinos

In 1937 Ettore Majorana proposed a particle that is its own antiparticle. For charged fermions this is impossible: the electron and positron carry opposite charges and cannot be identified. But the neutrino is electrically neutral. Nothing in principle forbids the identification of neutrino with antineutrino. The only distinction would be chirality itself.

If neutrinos are Majorana particles, then a "left-handed neutrino" and a "right-handed antineutrino" are the same object observed in different chiral projections. The particle does not need a distinct antiparticle. The charge conjugate of a left-chiral state is a right-chiral state, and both are aspects of a single self-conjugate field.

Open Question
Is the neutrino its own antiparticle?

The experimental signature is neutrinoless double beta decay (0νββ). Two neutrons decay simultaneously, emitting two electrons and no neutrinos. This violates lepton number by two units. If observed, it would prove the Majorana nature of neutrinos. Experiments including GERDA, LEGEND, nEXO and KamLAND-Zen have searched for decades. No confirmed signal exists.

The Dirac equation permits four species: left-chiral neutrino, right-chiral neutrino, left-chiral antineutrino, right-chiral antineutrino. The Majorana equation collapses this to two: left-chiral and right-chiral projections of one self-conjugate field. The missing degrees of freedom are not absent. They are redundant.

Chirality as the sole distinction

For a Majorana neutrino, chirality does all the work that charge conjugation does for electrons. The weak interaction couples to the left-chiral projection. The right-chiral projection exists but is sterile: invisible to W and Z bosons. The handedness of the weak force is not merely a property of the interaction. It is the definition of what we mean by "neutrino" versus "antineutrino".

03 — The Mass Mechanism

The See-Saw and the Missing Right

Neutrino oscillation experiments proved that neutrinos have mass. The masses are tiny: at least six orders of magnitude lighter than the electron. The Higgs field couples left-chiral to right-chiral fermions through Yukawa interactions. But if the right-chiral neutrino does not exist, or does not couple to the Higgs, the mechanism fails.

The see-saw mechanism resolves this. Add right-handed neutrinos to the Standard Model. Give them both a Dirac mass (coupling left to right via the Higgs) and a Majorana mass (self-coupling of the right-handed field, permitted because it carries no gauge charge). The resulting mass matrix has two eigenvalues whose product is fixed but whose ratio depends on the Majorana scale:

Mass matrix: ⎛ 0 mD ⎞ M = ⎝ mD MR ⎠ When MR ≫ mD the eigenvalues separate: light: mν ≈ mD2 / MR (the observed neutrino) heavy: MN ≈ MR (the sterile partner)

This is the see-saw: a mechanical metaphor for an algebraic inversion. The heavier the right-handed Majorana mass, the lighter the observed left-handed neutrino. The two ends of the plank move in opposite directions.

Open Question
What is the mass scale of the right-handed neutrino?

The see-saw formula has a degeneracy: different combinations of Dirac and Majorana masses can produce the same light neutrino mass. The right-handed neutrino could live at the GUT scale (1015 GeV), the electroweak scale (~100 GeV), or the keV scale. Only the third possibility is directly accessible to current experiments.

04 — Invisible Chirality

The Handedness of Dark Matter

Dark matter has mass and interacts gravitationally. It carries no electric charge. It does not couple to the strong force. Does dark matter possess chirality? If it is fermionic, it must. The question is which chirality dominates, and whether this connects to the left-handed bias of the visible sector.

The neutrino minimal Standard Model (νMSM) proposes a concrete answer. Three right-handed sterile neutrinos are added. Two generate the observed neutrino masses via the see-saw and produce baryon asymmetry through leptogenesis. The third, lighter and with a mass in the keV range, is the dark matter candidate.

Sterile neutrino dark matter

The lightest sterile neutrino in the νMSM is a right-handed Majorana fermion: it belongs to the (0,½) representation and carries no weak isospin or hypercharge. Its only coupling to the Standard Model is through a tiny mixing angle with the active neutrinos. It would decay radiatively into an active neutrino and a photon, producing a monoenergetic X-ray line detectable by space telescopes.

Open Question
Is dark matter fundamentally right-handed?

If the νMSM is correct, the visible universe is built from left-handed weak-interacting fermions and the invisible universe from their right-handed sterile counterparts. The handedness of the cosmos would be not a violation of symmetry but a sorting: left-handed matter interacts, right-handed matter gravitates.

The 3.5 keV X-ray line, tentatively detected in galaxy cluster observations in 2014, was proposed as the radiative decay signature of a ~7 keV sterile neutrino. Subsequent observations have been inconclusive. The question remains open.

Open Question
Does dark matter experience parity violation?

If dark matter has its own gauge interactions, those could also violate parity. A dark analogue of the weak force could couple exclusively to one chirality of dark fermions. The visible and dark sectors might share the same algebraic structure: SU(2)L here, SU(2)D there. Mirror matter models explore this possibility explicitly.

05 — The Photon Endgame

Thermodynamics of a Massless Universe

Consider the end state. All black holes have evaporated. All massive particles have decayed or been consumed. The universe contains only massless radiation. This is a photon gas seeking equilibrium in an ever-expanding volume.

Temperature ~3 K

The equation of state of a photon gas relates internal energy to pressure through U = 3PV. The photon gas can be written as PV = NkT, but with the average energy per photon equal to 2.7 kT rather than (3/2)kT for the classical ideal gas.

Photon gas Internal energy: U = aVT⁴ (Stefan-Boltzmann) Pressure: P = U / (3V) = (a/3) T⁴ Equation of state: PV = NkT with ⟨E⟩ per photon = 2.7 kT where a = 4σ/c = 8π⁵k⁴ / (15 h³c³) Ideal gas Internal energy: U = (3/2) NkT Equation of state: PV = NkT with ⟨E⟩ per particle = (3/2) kT Key difference: N is fixed for the ideal gas, variable for the photon gas. The photon gas adjusts its particle number to match V and T.

For the photon gas, the number of particles is not conserved. The chemical potential vanishes. In the cosmological context, as the Hubble volume expands, photons redshift. Their wavelengths stretch with the scale factor. The temperature falls as T ∝ 1/a(t).

Boltzmann's Bridge

From Planck, E = hν = hc/λ. Setting this equal to kT gives T = hc/(kλ). For microwaves at λ ~ 1 mm this gives T ~ 3 K: the present CMB temperature. The Boltzmann constant bridges the microscopic (single photon energy) to the macroscopic (temperature of the universe).

The quantity of energy is conserved. Its quality diminishes. The universe cools from material to immaterial, from massive to massless, from structured to uniform. The entropy increases as the number of photons multiplies through black hole evaporation, each Hawking photon carrying away a quantum of information from behind the horizon.

06 — Penrose's Conformal Cyclical Cosmology

The Last Pop of Hawking Radiation

Penrose's proposal begins with a simple observation. The very early universe and the very late universe share a property: both are dominated by massless particles. In the early universe, temperatures are so extreme that all particles are ultra-relativistic and effectively massless. In the far future, all massive particles have decayed or been consumed by black holes, and only radiation remains.

For massless particles, there is no proper time. A photon does not experience the passage of events. Without mass there are no oscillation periods on which to mark out events. Without periods there is no clock. Without a clock, the distinction between a finite interval and an infinite one becomes a matter of conformal rescaling.

Stellar era → 10¹⁴ years
Stars exhaust their fuel. White dwarfs cool. Neutron stars spin down. The universe darkens.
Black hole era → 10⁴⁰ years
Galactic remnants spiral into supermassive black holes. The universe is a sparse collection of isolated black holes in an expanding void.
Hawking evaporation → 10¹⁰⁰ years
Black holes radiate at a temperature inversely proportional to their mass. As they shrink, they heat up. The final burst is violent.
The last pop
The final black hole emits its last photon. The universe contains only massless radiation, redshifting indefinitely. Time, in any operational sense, ceases.
Conformal Rescaling

The physical metric g of spacetime determines both angles and distances. A conformal rescaling replaces g with ĝ = Ω² g, preserving the causal structure (light cones, angles) while changing distances. Massless fields do not care about distances, only about the light-cone structure. Their physics is conformally invariant.

Penrose's insight: the far future of one aeon can be conformally compressed. As the conformal factor Ω approaches zero at future infinity, the infinite expanse is "squashed" to a finite boundary surface. Conversely, the Big Bang singularity of the next aeon can be conformally stretched by a factor that diverges there. If the two boundaries match as smooth spacelike hypersurfaces, they can be identified. This is the crossover between aeons. The low-density, low-temperature radiation of the dying universe becomes, after rescaling, the high-density, high-temperature radiation of a new Big Bang. The physics crosses over because massless particles are indifferent to the rescaling.

The relativistic energy-momentum relation E² = (mc²)² + (pc)² For massive particles at rest (p = 0): E = mc² Combining with Planck: E = hν gives an intrinsic period T = h / mc² Mass provides the clock. For massless particles (m = 0): E = pc No rest frame. No oscillation period. No clock. Energy is proportional to momentum alone. The constant of proportionality is the speed of light.

This is the equation that makes the endgame concrete. When the last massive particle has decayed or been consumed by a black hole, every surviving entity satisfies E = pc. Energy and momentum become locked in a fixed ratio. The Noether symmetry conjugate to energy is time translation. The Noether symmetry conjugate to momentum is space translation. For massive particles these are independent: you can have a particle at rest (energy without momentum) or boost it (changing the ratio). For massless particles the ratio is fixed at c. The generators of time and space translation are no longer independent. Time is just as space.

Combining with Planck's relation, E = hν, and Boltzmann's, E = kT, the temperature of the photon gas sets the energy per particle. Those heat photons just keep redshifting, well beyond their present microwave wavelengths. The quantity of energy remains conserved. Its quality diminishes inexorably in cooling from material to immaterial radiation, in multiplying by number. When all the universe's energy is locked up only in massless photons of momentum, then because there is no inertial matter, E² = (mc²)² + (pc)² reduces to E = pc for photons of frequency ν. Only after the last pop of Hawking radiation is emitted from the last black hole is the energy of the universe once again proportional to its momentum.

The CCC Requirement

For the conformal matching to work, all massive particles must eventually decay into massless ones. The Standard Model does not guarantee this. The electron is absolutely stable as far as we know. Proton decay is predicted by GUT theories but has never been observed (limits exceed 10³⁴ years). Penrose acknowledges this as the most speculative element of CCC: it requires new physics in which mass itself is not permanent.

Open Question
Does the Weyl curvature hypothesis explain why entropy was low at the Big Bang?

The Weyl tensor (spacetime curvature not determined by matter content) vanishes at the Big Bang and diverges at black hole singularities. Low Weyl curvature means conformally flat spacetime: high order, low gravitational entropy. Black holes absorb entropy from the matter fields. Their evaporation releases it as thermal radiation, resetting the gravitational sector for the next aeon.

07 — The Final Identification

Coda

In the present universe, the weak interaction distinguishes chirality from helicity. It couples to left-chiral fields regardless of their helicity. A massive neutrino has both helicity states accessible in principle, even though it is born left-chiral. The distinction is real because mass is real. But in the Penrose endgame, after the last Hawking photon, there is no mass anywhere. Every particle is its own lightlike trajectory. Every spin projects uniquely onto its momentum. The weak interaction, which requires massive W and Z bosons to mediate, has long since frozen out.

Open Question
Does the universe's chirality survive the loss of mass?

Parity violation is a consequence of the Higgs mechanism. SU(2)L × U(1)Y is broken by the Higgs vacuum expectation value. If, in the far future, all Higgs condensates have dissipated and the electroweak symmetry is restored, then the distinction between left and right chirality as a dynamical property may dissolve. The handedness that built our amino acids may be a feature of the broken phase, not of the underlying theory.

Open Question
Is the next aeon left-handed too?

If CCC is correct, the massless radiation of the far future seeds the next Big Bang. As the new aeon cools, the weak interaction must again pick a chirality. Is this choice preserved across the conformal boundary? The gravitational wave polarisation patterns imprinted on the CMB carry information about the previous aeon. If those patterns encode a chiral preference, the handedness of the next aeon's biology may already be determined.

The gravitational waves emanated at the final stages carry maximal thermodynamic entropy. Their polarisation states combine into circular modes that carry helicity ±2. In the massless endgame, these graviton helicity states are also chirality states. The gravitational sector, too, loses the distinction.

The Primordial Filter

The circularly polarised radiation of the early universe, whether electromagnetic or gravitational, passed through the pre-biotic medium and selected for one enantiomer over the other. The left-handedness of amino acids, the right-handedness of DNA, the parity violation of the weak force: all expressions of a single asymmetry, frozen in at the moment the universe first broke its own symmetry.

Only in 10100 years, when E² = (mc²)² + (pc)² has shed its first term for every particle in the cosmos, will time be just as space: the symmetry conjugate to, and generator of energy indistinguishable from the symmetry conjugate to, and generator of momentum. Only with that last pop, when gravitational waves emanate with maximal thermodynamic entropy, will the universe's chirality be one and the same as its helicity. Massless once more, as it was without inertia at the Big Bang.
